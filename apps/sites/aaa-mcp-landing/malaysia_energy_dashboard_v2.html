⚠️ Critical Gap: Domestic vs. Exported Crude
Malaysia exported RM23.8B in crude + condensate (mainly to Thailand, Australia, Japan) but also imported RM54.1B in crude (mainly from Saudi Arabia). This "import while exporting" pattern exists because refiners need specific crude grades. The 350,000 boepd figure from industry chat likely refers to crude-only production available for domestic refinery feedstock — not total hydrocarbon production. This is a critical distinction the government has not clarified publicly.

⚠️ Critical: Saudi Arabia is Malaysia's #1 Crude Supplier
Malaysia imports RM18B of crude from Saudi Arabia — and Pengerang is a JV with Saudi Aramco. This means Malaysia's largest crude import source is directly tied to our largest refinery investment. When Hormuz disrupts Saudi crude flow, BOTH Malaysia's import supply AND the Pengerang JV are simultaneously affected. This is not coincidence — it's structural exposure.
🛢️ The Refined Product Paradox
Why does Malaysia export AND import refined products simultaneously?
As Professor Dr Yeah Kim Leng explained: Malaysia produces high-quality Tapis Blend light sweet crude. Instead of refining it all locally, Malaysia exports the premium crude and imports refined products Singapore specializes in. Singapore refineries are optimised for Middle Eastern heavy-sour crude — different grade from Tapis. This is a "buy cheap, sell high" trade strategy but creates import dependency for refined products.

Renegotiate Pengerang JV — Domestic Crude Priority
Saudi Arabia is Malaysia's #1 crude supplier at RM18B/year. Pengerang is a JV with Saudi Aramco. If Saudi Arabia needs crude more than Malaysia needs refined product, Aramco's incentive is export, not domestic supply. A domestic supply guarantee clause — first call on product at Malaysian market price — would convert Pengerang from "import refinery" to genuine domestic security asset.
Source: DOSM trade data, PETRONAS Activity Outlook 2024-2026

Build Strategic Crude Reserve
Malaysia has zero disclosed strategic petroleum reserve. DOSM: 2025 production = 183.6M bbl/year = ~503K boepd. A 30-day reserve = ~15M barrels. At US$70/bbl = ~US$1B. This is achievable via offshore FPSO charter within 6-12 months. Petronas has the expertise. The only missing ingredient is political decision.
Source: DOSM production data, PETRONAS Activity Outlook 2024-2026

New PSC awards for Sabah deepwater — already identified in PETRONAS Activity Outlook 2024-2026 as target — should be expedited. Discovery to first oil = 5-8 years minimum. Start now.
Source: DOSM Q4 2025, PETRONAS Activity Outlook 2026-2028
5
Activate LNG Import Infrastructure Now
