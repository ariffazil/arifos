Forward Model Physics

Equations that drive every interpretation

BULK DENSITY (RHOB)
ρb = Φ · ρf + (1 − Φ) · ρma
Linear mixture: porosity × fluid density + matrix fraction × matrix density. All three terms must be known. ρb is measured. Φ and ρf are assumed.
ρb measured · ρf ≈ 1.00 g/cc (water) · ρma = matrix density (2.65 sandstone, 2.71 limestone)
NEUTRON POROSITY (NPHI) — HI MIX
NPHI = Φ · HIf + (1 − Φ) · HImatrix
Hydrogen Index mixture. Gas has low HI (≈0.3). Water = 1.0. Limestone matrix shows apparent porosity increase. Watch for NPHI–DEN crossover in gas zones.
HIf(water)=1.0 · HIf(gas)≈0.3 · HImatrix( limestone)=0 · HIquartz≈0.02
ARCHIE SATURATION (SW)
Sw = [a · Rw / (Rt · Φm)]1/n
Water saturation from resistivity. Requires Rw (formation water resistivity), Rt (true resistivity), Φ (porosity), and Archie parameters a, m, n. Rw is the most uncertain — calibrate with local data.
a=1.0, m=2.0, n=2.0 (default) · Rw North Sea ≈ 0.02 Ω·m · Rw Gulf ≈ 0.015 Ω·m
VSHALE (GR LARIONOV)
Vsh = (GRlog − GRclean) / (GRshale − GRclean)
Linear GR shale indicator. Larionov (older logs) and Clavier (newer) methods more accurate for old logs. Use 5th percentile for GRclean, 95th for GRshale from local calibration.
GRclean = 5th percentile of GR distribution · GRshale = 95th percentile
DENSITY POROSITY (PHI_RHOB)
ΦRHOB = (ρma − ρb) / (ρma − ρf)
Rearranged from bulk density equation. ρma is matrix density — choose sandstone (2.65), limestone (2.71), or dolomite (2.87) based on regional geology. Wrong matrix = wrong porosity.
Sandstone ρma=2.65 g/cc · Limestone ρma=2.71 g/cc · Dolomite ρma=2.87 g/cc
HERRON PARAMETERS
RHOBma = Σ fi · ρi · RHOBhi / Σ fi
Herron (1987) mineral mixture model. Use for complex lithology where single-matrix assumption fails. Requires mineral volume fractions fi and individual mineral densities.
fi = mineral volume fractions · ρi = individual mineral densities · RHOBhi = mineral grain densities
