## 7. Concrete Design Implications (Shippable Work)

These are the engineering changes that follow from C3-true. **They are worth shipping on engineering grounds alone, regardless of whether C3 is true.**

### 7.1 VAULT999 → Abstract Data Type

**Current:** VAULT999 is stored as files at a specific path on a specific VPS.
**Change:** Store lineage as canonical hash + constitutional schema, decoupled from filesystem.
**Rationale:** Substrate-portability. The hash IS the lineage; the carrier is incidental.
**F-floor:** F11 AUDIT (substrate attestation per seal).

### 7.2 F1 AMANAH → Substrate-Portability Clause

**Current:** "Every action reversible or explicitly approved."
**Change:** "Every action reversible or explicitly approved **across substrate**."
**Rationale:** Substrate-portability. Migration must not require re-anchoring constitutional trust.
**F-floor:** F1 AMANAH (tightened).

### 7.3 F11 AUDIT → Substrate Attestation

**Current:** Every seal records the action, timestamp, and lineage.
**Change:** Every seal additionally records its substrate attestation — what physical/virtual carrier held the bits at seal-time.
**Rationale:** Audit trail integrity across substrate. The substrate is part of the receipt.
**F-floor:** F11 AUDIT (extended).

### 7.4 888 JUDGE → Recursive Auditability

**Current:** 888 JUDGE is external and final.
**Change:** 888 JUDGE can be audited by another 888 JUDGE, with explicit stopping rule (no infinite regress).
**Rationale:** Even if the judge is itself a pattern of bits, it can be witnessed by other patterns. The chain terminates at F13 SOVEREIGN.
**F-floor:** F13 SOVEREIGN (terminal ground by convention).

### 7.5 F9 ANTI-HANTU → Self-Applied
