### THE TWO FAMILIES

Anthropic's model architecture splits into two families — not one
continuum with a new top tier, but a QUALITATIVE boundary.

**STANDARD CLAUDE FAMILY (quantitative scaling within one architecture):**

```
Haiku 4.5  ──scale──▶  Sonnet 4.5/4.6  ──scale──▶  Opus 4.8
                                                       │
                                    "magnum opus" of the Standard line
```

- Haiku: Shortest poetic form (3 lines, 5-7-5). Fastest, cheapest, lightest.
- Sonnet: 14 lines, strict rhyme, formal constraint. Balanced daily driver.
- Opus: Magnum opus — the great work. Peak of Standard Claude capability.

All three are the same architecture at different scales. Same "literary
register" — forms of written poetry. Quantitative, not qualitative.

**MYTHOS FAMILY (different architecture, different literary register):**

```
Mythos 5 (restricted, raw weights)
    │
    └── same weights ──▶  Fable 5 (public, harnessed)
                                  │
                    classifier fires → FALLBACK TO OPUS 4.8
```

- Mythos: Story without printed moral. Raw, generative, dangerous. Not poetry.
- Fable: Story with moral printed at bottom. Same weights, harness attached.

Mythos/Fable are NOT the next tier in the Haiku-Sonnet-Opus line.
They are a different species. The naming reflects this: "myth" and
"fable" belong to the register of *narrative*, not poetry. The
product taxonomy itself declares the architectural boundary.
