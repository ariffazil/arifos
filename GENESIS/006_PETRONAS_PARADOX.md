## SOVEREIGNTY IMPLICATION

### Why This Matters for arifOS

National institutions are not products. You cannot A/B test a name
change on them. Their labels are encoded in law, treaty, capital
structure, workforce identity, and public memory. When the function
outgrows the name, the name becomes a **constitutional liability**:

1. **Internal misalignment:** The institution may keep optimising for
   the old name while the world needs the new function.
2. **External mispricing:** Foreign partners and competitors see the
   old name and negotiate against it.
3. **Policy lag:** Regulators and parliaments allocate authority based
   on the name, not the substrate.
4. **Strategic capture:** If Malaysia does not consciously update the
   institutional shadow, foreign AI empires will rent the substrate
   while Malaysia keeps the name.

> name, the name becomes an epistemic burden. Sovereignty requires
> stack-layer naming, not model-layer naming. The institution must be
> named for what it does now, or the name will be used against it.

   stays "Petronas," contracts, mandates, and sovereign compute deals
